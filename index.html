Who are we

General

ByteHacks is a student-led hackathon that will allow students to create mobile apps or websites that solve real-world problems. It aims to spark an interest in technology and problem-solving within middle and high school students.

Why us?

ByteHacks has the goal of introducing technology to students, so as a sponsor, you will have the opportunity to promote your company’s brand. Due to a large number of hackers participating in our hackathon, you will be able to advertise your products so that hackers may use your tools in future projects.  ByteHacks will be 4 days long and will take place from July 31st to August 3rd online. We open up our hackathon to all middle and high school students of various coding levels and ages.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event during which students collaborate together to turn their ideas into projects.

What if I don’t know how to code?

ByteHacks welcomes programmers of all levels. Hackathons are a great opportunity to learn because there will be plenty of workshops and mentors to aid you in your project.

What are the project criterias?

Participants are free to build anything, including mobile applications, websites, hardware projects, etc. Rubic to the rubric for more information regarding the project criteria.

Who can attend?

Students in 6th to 12th grade are welcome to join ByteHacks.

How much does it cost to attend?

Thanks to our wonderful sponsors, ByteHacks is free to all participants!

What are the group sizes?

A group can consist of 1-4 participants. Don’t worry if you do not already have a team. We will be hosting a meet and greet for participants to talk with other participants and form teams.
